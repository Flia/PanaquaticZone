UNIFIED PANAQUATIC GROWING ZONE



(If you couldn't guess I was [i]really[/i] lost for a good name for this mod.)
So what's this about? I wanted to finally attempt a proper [url=steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3026235711]Merren[/url]-centered walkthrough, only to realize that its two cute plants are locked behind not [url=steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2748889667]one[/url], but [b][url=steamcommunity.com/workshop/filedetails/?id=2134308522]two[/url][/b], uh, content-quantity-exploding mods. I love Vanilla Expanded series as much as the next person in the workshop, but with that amount of plants I'm bound to develop a new, never-before-seen allergy.
So I searched for a mod which might be adding an Aquatic Zones on its own. I found [url=steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3217394931][LTS]Biosphere[/url], which looked nice enough until I realized that both Biosphere and VPE lock their zones to freshwater.
"But nay," I cried, "what about my mermaid paradise built in the middle of a crescent island, surrounded only by the purest, most turquoise of brinewaters?"
So I made this mod. First I've ever done.
[h1]Features[/h1]
By itself, the mod only adds the Panaquatic Zone and its corresponding gizmo. It is a [strike]very[/strike] moderately clever gizmo. Depending on the mod settings (there are settings) it can ignore or cover ocean tiles, and ignore or cover polluted tiles. By default, ocean tiles are allowed, polluted are not. When selected, the zone will only show you plants that can grow somewhere in the selected zone. And if it is indeed only part of the zone and not the whole zone that will be sown - for example, because you are trying to sow a freshwater plant where the river delta mixes with ocean tiles - it will warn you about it.
"This is all very neat," you say. "But why does the list of plants only show me a potato?" Well, that is because in order for anything useful to show up you have to install another mod which adds water plants. Currently I overtly support three:
[*][url=steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3026235711]Goji's Fantasy Race: Merren[/url], which has only two plants, Quackweed and Fibrekelp. However, I graciously allowed them to grow both in freshwater and saltwater. Because it made sense for merfolk agriculture. Oh and you do not require Vanilla Plants mods for them to show up anymore, I remove that requirement with a patch.
[*][url=steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2748889667]Vanilla Plants Expanded - More Plants[/url], which adds Taro, Water Chestnut, Watercress, and a crop variety of Lotus that is different from Odyssey's lotus. To keep with the original intent (and common sense) all of those plants only grow in freshwater. I also remove their zone gizmo so it won't crowd the menu. If you don't like that, you can delete the XML patch - I'm sorry, while I wanted to make it a mod setting I never figured out how.
[*][url=steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3217394931][LTS]Biosphere[/url] which... I'm gonna be honest, I am not entirely certain what was the intent with some of these. Aquaflume only grows in mud and two varieties of fresh water; Cattail has similar spread but can unexpectedly also grow in ocean tiles; Goblets are a small pain to grow because they [i]only[/i] grow in fresh, clean, still water. This works strangely even with the mod's own aquatic zone; still, I didn't want to mess with another mod, so those requirements stay as they were. Red Rice and Mistveil at least have no wildTerrainTags, so I gave them the same treatment as VPE stuff. While on the topic of VPE - I remove Biosphere's gizmo too, with the same solution of killing the patch file if you want it back.
[*]If you have Odyssey, you will see its variant of Lotus added to the list. Well, [i]maybe[/i] you'll see it. I didn't define a separate cultivar def, and so it's a bit picky about where it grows.
[h1]Technical[/h1]
I leverage the system of water body types (None, Freshwater, Saltwater, Other), which as far as I can tell was added specifically for Odyssey fishing. Luckily for us this system is part of Core so I can use that data to figure out whether a growing zone is able to be placed without making Odyssey a dependency. My logic is: water that supports fish should support plantlife, right? 
If you'd like to make an aquatic plant that can be sown in Panaquatic Zone (wow, thanks! Tell me of your mod) you only need to follow these simple steps:
[*]Make sure your plant is a viable water plant. Looking at Odyssey plants is a good idea, but mainly it's just setting <completelyIgnoreFertility>true</completelyIgnoreFertility> in the XML.
[*]To available sowTags, add "Panaquatic_Zone".
[*]Optionally: you can use Mod Extension to specify plant preference. Currently, this mod supports [i]Saltwater[/i], [i]Freshwater[/i], and [i]Either[/i]. However, if your plant has wildTerrainTags it will be ignored - make a cultivar def first! If you have neither mod extension defined nor any wild tags, it will default to freshwater.
[h1]Possible issues[/h1]
[*]If none of the three mods above are in your modlist, the default plant will be the humble potato. Which of course cannot be grown in water, but what behavior to have in this situation I don't know.
[*]Mods which add water tiles, especially buildable variety, may interact funny. I tried to account for that but I'm a novice, so [strike]give me a break[/strike] report any issues so I might refine this.
[*]Since my zone inherits from vanilla Growing Zone, mods which affect it should affect Panaquatic too. On one hand, this allows for Smart Farming. On the other, right-clicking on Growing Zone gizmo and hiding or unhiding zones will also do so for my Panaquatic zones. If there's a lot of feedback that this is inconvenient, I will think about rewriting my code, but for now I am tired boss
