So what's this about? I wanted to finally attempt a proper [url=steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3026235711]Merren[/url]-centered walkthrough, only to realize that its two cute plants are locked behind not [url=steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2748889667]one[/url], but [b][url=steamcommunity.com/workshop/filedetails/?id=2134308522]two[/url][/b], uh, content-quantity-exploding mods. I love VE series as much as the next person in the workshop, but with that amount of plants I'm bound to develop a new, never-before-seen allergy.
So I searched for a mod which might be adding an Aquatic Growing Zones on its own. I found [url=steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3217394931][LTS]Biosphere[/url], which looked nice enough until I realized that both Biosphere and VE lock their zones to freshwater.
"But nay," I cried, "what about my mermaid paradise built in the middle of a crescent island, surrounded only by the purest, most turquoise of brinewaters?"
So I made this mod. First I've ever done.
[h1]Technical[/h1]
I leverage the system of water body types (None, Freshwater, Saltwater, Other) used for Odyssey fishing. Luckily for us this system is part of Core so I can use that data without making Odyssey a dependency. My logic is: water that supports fish should support plantlife, right? 
Currently I connect that system to plant logic by simply adding custom terrain tags to both water tile defs and plant defs on startup. However, I am a rookie at modding and that system may yet change. If you'd like to see support for more mods, including your own, comment and I should look at it. 
[h1]Features[/h1]
By itself, the mod only adds the Panaquatic Zone and its corresponding gizmo. It is a [strike]very[/strike] moderately clever gizmo. Depending on the mod settings (there are settings) it can ignore or cover ocean tiles, and ignore or cover polluted tiles. By default, ocean tiles are allowed, polluted are not. When selected, the zone will only show you plants that can grow somewhere in the selected zone. And if it is indeed only part of the zone and not the whole zone that will be sown - for example, because you are trying to sow a freshwater plant where the river delta mixes with ocean tiles - it will warn you about it.
"This is all very neat," you say. "But why does the list of plants only show me a potato?" Well, that is because in order for anything useful to show up you have to install another mod which adds water plants. Currently I overtly support these options:
[*][url=steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3026235711]Goji's Fantasy Race: Merren[/url], which has only two plants, Quackweed and Fibrekelp. However, I graciously allowed them to grow both in freshwater and saltwater. Because it made sense for merfolk agriculture. Oh and you do not require Vanilla Plants mods for them to show up anymore, I remove that requirement with a patch.
[*][url=steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2748889667]Vanilla Plants Expanded - More Plants[/url], which adds Taro, Water Chestnut, Watercress, and a crop variety of Lotus that is different from Odyssey's lotus. To keep with the original intent (and common sense) all of those plants only grow in freshwater. I also remove their zone gizmo so it won't crowd the menu.
[*][url=steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2963220393]Simple Algae - 'VPE More Plants' Addon[/url] is also supported. Same as Merren plants, it can grow either in saltwater or freshwater. You also do not actually require Vanilla Plants mods, although it will still appear as red in the modlist.
[*][url=steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3217394931][LTS]Biosphere[/url] which... I'm gonna be honest, I am not entirely certain what was the intent with some of these, since there are some quirks even when you use the mod's own aquatic zone. Still, I didn't want to mess with another mod, so Aquaflume, Cattail, and Goblets have odd terrain requirements. Red Rice and Mistveil at least have no wildTerrainTags, so I gave them the same treatment as VPE stuff. While on topic of VPE - I remove Biosphere's gizmo too.
[*]Yes, true to the preview, if you have Odyssey you will see its variant of Lotus added to the list. Well, [i]maybe[/i] you'll see it. I didn't define a separate cultivar def, and so it's a bit picky about where it grows.
[h1]Possible issues[/h1]
[*]If no plants are available for sowing, it will be "stuck" showing a plant that you cannot select. It's not an error but rather a design issue I didn't figure out.
[*]Mods which add water tiles, especially buildable variety, may do funnies. I tried to account for that but I'm a novice, so [strike]give me a break[/strike] report any issues so I might refine this.
[*]Since my zone inherits from vanilla Growing Zone, mods which affect Growing should affect Panaquatic too. On one hand, this allows for Smart Farming to apply. On the other, right-clicking on Growing Zone gizmo and hiding or unhiding zones will also do so for my Panaquatic zones. If there's a lot of feedback that this is inconvenient, I will think about rewriting my code, but for now I am tired boss
